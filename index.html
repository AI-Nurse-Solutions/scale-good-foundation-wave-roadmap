Scale Good Foundation Wave Roadmap

The Nurse Intelligence Network is moving the first five Scale Good initiatives from visionary portfolio language into governed, measurable, nurse-led development.

Reality-first posture: this roadmap does not claim that these initiatives are clinically validated, funded, certified, legally incorporated, or ready for unsupervised patient care. It defines the first governed path toward credible pilots.
View the five initiatives Download Markdown source
Governing objective

Scale beneficial innovation without sacrificing humanity.

NIN’s expansion posture is federated, locally legitimate, and centrally governed. Local nurses and communities must shape implementation. Central governance protects safety, standards, measurement, and trust.

Operating interpretation

  • NIN is the operating organization and global professional network.
  • Scale Good is the umbrella initiative portfolio.
  • NAIO supports standards, certification, and governance.
  • EDENA provides ethical classification and control logic.
  • Florence ecosystem may enable appropriate initiatives, but is not required for every program.
Why start here

The full portfolio has 13 projects. The foundation wave builds the operating base first.

These five initiatives create the minimum infrastructure for standards, learning, safety, local leadership, and nurse-led building before heavier clinical, data, robotics, or distributed infrastructure work advances.

Portfolio discipline

What advances later

Data cooperatives, clinical co-pilots, robotics governance, sovereign infrastructure, and real-time enforcement engines remain important, but they require stronger technical, legal, privacy, funding, and evidence foundations.

Remaining portfolio for review

Eight initiatives remain in the Scale Good review queue.

These projects are still part of the 13-world-project portfolio. They are not removed. They are held for readiness review because they require stronger data governance, technical validation, institutional integration, funding, legal review, safety evidence, or regulatory clarity before public pilot claims.

02 · Capability review

EDENA Sentient Shield

Real-time boundary enforcement for drift, scope creep, and ethical violations. Requires technical definition, sandbox testing, auditability, and validation before care-adjacent use.

03 · Capability review

Nurse Data Cooperative and Ethics Council

Nurse- and community-governed data use. Requires legal structure, consent model, privacy review, data rights, and cross-border governance before any real data work.

04 · Advanced infrastructure

Florence Sovereign Mesh

Nurse-controlled distributed compute infrastructure. Requires architecture, security model, operating capacity, node governance, capital plan, and threat modeling.

05 · Capability review

Judgment Vault and Knowledge Graph

Provenance-rich capture of nursing judgment. Can begin as non-PHI knowledge capture, but needs ontology, authorship, review, licensing, and clinical-use boundaries.

06 · Advanced infrastructure

Compassion Engine

Constraint architecture for preserving therapeutic presence and relational care. Needs construct definition, cultural review, evidence basis, and safeguards against false empathy claims.

08 · Capability review

Simulation and ICU Co-Pilot

Simulation can proceed separately under bounded conditions. Bedside co-pilot work is high-risk and requires clinical governance, validation, legal review, and institutional approval.

12 · Capability review

Climate-Health Sentinel Network

Nurse-led climate-health monitoring. Should start with public data and local review before prediction, alerting, or public-health action claims.

13 · Advanced infrastructure

Nurse Robotics Governance Authority

Governance standards for clinical robotics. Should remain a research and standards-mapping track until use cases, partners, regulatory intersections, and nurse oversight roles are defined.

Review principle: these initiatives may advance after they complete independent readiness assessment, EDENA tiering, owner assignment, evidence review, and a clear implementation path. Visionary value does not substitute for operational readiness.

Safety posture

Agents propose. Humans judge. Nurses steward.

Scale Good uses EDENA-style risk classification to protect patients, practitioners, communities, and institutional trust.

Green

Education and planning

Public research, community-building, workshops, and non-sensitive planning may proceed under standard review.

Yellow

Prototypes and pilots

Simulation, de-identified data, professional materials, and operational pilots require named human owners, monitoring, and escalation pathways.

Red

Clinical or high-stakes use

PHI, patient-specific recommendations, autonomous actions, robotics, safety claims, or material care impact require explicit approval, auditability, legal review, and institutional governance.

First 90 days

From approved wave to governed pilots.

The first 90 days are for control, discovery, and pilot preparation — not premature scale.

Days 1–30

Confirm hierarchy, assign a portfolio steward, create a decision forum, open evidence folders, and draft charters for the five initiatives.

Days 31–60

Score all 13 initiatives, complete the Philippine Bridge discovery plan, design the first Context Bundle pilot, draft the CAIRLN taxonomy, and produce Nightingale Protocol v0.1.

Days 61–90

Select which initiatives are ready for G2 pilot preparation, identify owners and partners, prepare the funder narrative, and define the NIN Summit 2026 role.

Public claim boundary

This roadmap is a commitment to governed development.

It is not a claim that the initiatives are already operational at scale.

This roadmap does not claim any initiative is currently:

  • Clinically validated.
  • Legally incorporated as a separate entity.
  • Certified by a regulator.
  • Authorized for unsupervised patient care.
  • Funded or approved by a health system.
  • Operational across countries.
  • Protected by trademark or intellectual property filings.
